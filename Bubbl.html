Challenge

Young adults want to spend time with friends but struggle with the logistics of planning.

Traditional methods, like group chats and scheduling apps, require too much coordination and effort. Busy schedules lead to frequent cancellations, and the fear of rejection discourages users from reaching out.

Existing platforms, such as Instagram and Life360 allow users to connect digitally but fail to provide a seamless way to initiate real-world interactions.

Results

By seamlessly integrating social interactions into daily life, Bubbl helps users spend more time with friends without the stress of planning.

It allows users to create open invites, see what their friends are doing in real-time, and join activities without the pressure of direct invitations.

User testing showed that Bubbl significantly reduced planning friction, encouraged spontaneous socializing, and minimized the anxiety of rejection.

Following a framework to understand the user's need and address it, we came up with the initial question:

"How might we use elements of everyday life to promote bonding and relationship-building?"

Young adults struggle to maintain in-person friendships due to the high effort required for planning and scheduling conflicts.

In user research, 70% of participants reported that they frequently canceled or postponed hangouts due to scheduling difficulties, and 60% expressed social anxiety around inviting friends out due to the fear of rejection.

After summarizing the user's pain and gain points into below key takeaways, we refined our approach with the question:

"How might we make planning in-person gatherings easy and fit into busy people's every day schedules?"

Realizing the gaps and limitations in current alternatives, we approached user testing with a mid-fi prototype before further iterations.

Bubbl is a social coordination platform designed to make meeting up with friends effortless and stress-free.

Instead of requiring users to schedule events in advance, Bubbl integrates social interactions into daily routines, allowing spontaneous and low-stress planning.

Users can create and join a Bubbl effortlessly, while being updated on live events in their social circles.

As seen in the demo, Bubbl's open invite structure reduces the effort required to organize hangouts, encourages more frequent and meaningful connections, and helps users build stronger relationships in their busy schedules.

Takeaway & Next Steps

What We Learned

The research and user testing confirmed that young adults want a low-effort way to maintain friendships without the complications of traditional planning. Bubbl successfully lowers the barriers to hanging out by making the process spontaneous, stress-free, and integrated into daily life.

Future Improvements

Based on user feedback, future iterations of Bubbl will focus on:

  1. Enhancing event pages with richer details, such as activity descriptions and shared preferences.

  2. Exploring additional features like group recommendations based on mutual interests and availability.

Conclusion

Bubbl provides a practical and effective solution to a common problem among young adults: the difficulty of planning and maintaining social interactions. By reducing the effort required to organize hangouts, Bubbl encourages more frequent and meaningful connections, helping users build stronger relationships in an increasingly busy world.
